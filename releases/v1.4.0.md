## Position

`v1.4.0` is the tapped sprint execution-control and UX compliance-escrow cut.

This release adds sprint as a bounded execution-control surface for pricing
proof cost, batching work, and forcing close review under existing
constitutional authority. It also adds the detailed UX use case: short UX
sprints may defer local compliance proof as visible escrow when per-change
proof cost exceeds expected correction cost.

## Material Changes

`SPEC_METHOD.md` now defines a sprint as a bounded execution-control surface,
not a new constitutional layer. A sprint may batch tickets or iteration
entries, price proof cost, and force close review under existing Goals,
Intent, Product Definition, Requirements, and Design authority. It cannot
create, supersede, weaken, or override that authority.

`SPEC_METHOD.md` now makes proof-cost economics explicit. Sprints are lawful
when deferred compliance remains local, visible, bounded, and repayable. A
sprint may be recommended for close when the timebox expires, the scope is
reached, accumulated changed surface area makes review cheaper than continued
iteration, review uncertainty rises, or discovered gaps show the sprint
boundary is no longer the right container.

`TICKET_METHOD.md` now adds `.ai-workspace/sprints/` as the durable execution
batch and sprint-close review layer. Sprint manifests carry authority refs,
scope, excluded boundaries, expected change classes, included tickets or
iterations, closure trigger, closure law, proof surface, deferred compliance,
non-closure conditions, and paydown policy.

`TICKET_METHOD.md` now defines sprint status as `active`, `closing`, `closed`,
or `superseded`. Sprint close moves through forensic review: freeze new
iteration, inspect each included ticket or iteration entry, check evidence
durability, walk changed surfaces against cited authority, classify deferred
compliance, and open paydown tickets where debt must survive the close review.

`TICKET_METHOD.md` now defines sprint compliance escrow. Local proof, visual
review, accessibility cleanup, replay proof, design wording cleanup, trace
cleanup, and local realization cleanup may be deferred only when the governing
method allows it and the sprint close review must classify and pay down the
debt. Product truth, data contracts, runtime/governance semantics, missing
constructive carriers, public graph functions, `AssetSurface` contracts, and
law-changing work cannot be hidden in escrow.

`UX_METHOD.md` now identifies UX as the primary governed use case for sprint
compliance escrow. UX sprints may defer screenshots, walkthrough capture,
visual review, copy/layout cleanup, accessibility remediation, Msg-replay proof
updates, design-module wording, and trace cleanup during a short iteration
window.

`UX_METHOD.md` also states what UX sprints may not escrow:
product-truth-changing messages without admitted carriers, new or changed
typed product contracts, product-meaningful navigation or selection authority,
runtime/governance/closure/continuation/provenance/evidence semantics, release
criteria, or method law.

UX sprint close is now a forensic walkthrough. Each changed screen, state, and
product-meaningful interaction is classified as `accepted`, `local_paydown`,
`design_reframe`, `requirement_reprice`, `product_reprice`, or `remove`.
Method closure still requires Msg-replay proof and accessibility obligations
unless an explicit exemption is recorded.

`strategy/sprint_aggregation.md` now records that the narrower sprint
execution-control model has graduated into the live standards. The strategy
note remains background only where it differs from the ratified method.
